# Timeline & Historical Fact Sheet

Two layers: the **historical spine** (dated events the book is built around, with sources) and the **book-internal chronology** (invented events with derived dates). When drafting, check both — most continuity errors in a ten-year story are date errors.

### Other sources worth having in reach

- **Philo, *Embassy to Gaius* 299–305** — the gilded-shields episode (not in the outline, but its texture is useful) and the hostile character sketch of Pilate: inflexible, stubborn, vindictive. Useful as the *reputation* Pontius knows he has.
- **Luke 13:1** — "the Galileans whose blood Pilate mingled with their sacrifices." No other record; the book can quietly identify this with the colonnade riot (Ch10) or leave it as one more rumor.
- **Pilate's coinage, A.D. 29–31** — bore pagan implements (lituus, simpulum); background texture for Ch9–15 friction.
- **"Claudia Procula"** — her name comes from later tradition (e.g., the *Gospel of Nicodemus*), not the New Testament. The book's use of "Claudia" alone is clean.

## Known wrinkles (decided: tolerate, unless drafting makes them worse)

1. **Ch2 betrothal scene (~A.D. 18)** has the guardian saying there is "talk of a provincial appointment… once the current governor of Judaea steps down" — but Gratus had been in post only ~3 years then and served until 26. Read generously it's vague patronage gossip, not a prediction. Don't sharpen it in revision; if anything, soften "steps down" to eastern-posting talk generally.
2. **The aqueduct riot has no source date.** The book's ~28–29 placement is a choice; keep all later references consistent with it (Ch11's aftermath letters predate the Sejanus fall by ~2 years).
3. **Ages of Marcus** — "boy" at the trial (A.D. 33) vs. steward duties at Ostia (26). Resolved in CHARACTERS.md: born ~A.D. 10, and "boy" doubles as slave-address. Keep any Ch3–29 references consistent with that.
